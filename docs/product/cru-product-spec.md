# 1. Vision & Mission

## Vision

The wine industry has a gatekeeping problem. The best bottles, the best stories, the best buying experiences are reserved for people with the right connections, the right zip code, or the willingness to spend $200 to feel like they belong. Meanwhile, the people actually making the wine — farmers with calloused hands and generational knowledge — never asked for any of that pretension. Cru exists to tear down the wall between great wine and the people who'd love it if anyone bothered to introduce them properly.

## Mission

Cru is a wine discovery and marketplace platform that replaces the industry's broken gatekeeping model with intelligent, personal curation — powered by real expertise and smart technology. We connect curious drinkers with wines worth caring about at prices they actually want to pay, from stores they can actually walk into. For collectors and enthusiasts, we build the infrastructure they've never had: a liquid marketplace, professional cellaring, investment tools, and a community built around the glass, not the price tag.

# 2. Problem Space

## What's Broken

**For consumers** — Buying wine is either mindless or intimidating. There's no accessible middle path. You either grab whatever's on the shelf or you need to perform wine literacy to feel welcome. Existing platforms offer ratings (Vivino's 4.2 stars) or algorithms, but none replicate the thing that actually converts someone from casual drinker to curious explorer: a trusted person who says "you need to try this, here's why."

**For producers** — The winemakers doing the most interesting, careful work have the least visibility. Mass-market brands dominate shelf space and search results. Small producers get buried, and their stories — which are the actual product — never reach the consumer.

**For enthusiasts/collectors** — Tools are fragmented across a dozen platforms. Track your cellar here, bid at auction there, insure somewhere else. No unified experience exists for managing a collection as both a passion and an asset.

## The Trigger Moment

Someone travels. They sit at a table in a small town, drink something transcendent, and feel the gap between that experience and the one they get at home. Cru captures that moment and builds a permanent bridge back to it.

## Why Now

The wine industry is in a commercial downturn — traditional models are failing. Three forces are converging:

**Generational palate shift** — Millennials and Gen Z don't buy wine the way their parents did. They want stories, sustainability, and discovery over scores and brand recognition. The natural wine movement proves it: "natural" means "cared for" and "non-commercial" to this consumer, and they're spending real money on it.

**Post-COVID e-commerce permanence** — Drizly shut down in March 2025, but the behavior it created didn't die. Consumers are comfortable buying alcohol online. There's a supply-side gap and a demand-side habit, with no one owning the experience layer.

**AI-powered personalization at cost** — The technology to deliver "expert friend at scale" — taste profiling, semantic understanding of preferences, contextual recommendations — is finally good enough and cheap enough to build on.

# 3. Target Personas

## Persona 1: The Explorer (Primary — Day 1 Target)

**Who:** 28-42, urban/suburban professional. Drinks wine weekly but couldn't tell you what "Côtes du Rhône" means. Knows they like "that red from the restaurant" but can't find it at the store. Spends $12-35/bottle.

**Motivation:** "I want to drink better wine without becoming a wine person."

**Current behavior:** Grabs by label recognition, price, or Vivino score. Feels vaguely guilty about not knowing more. Intimidated by wine shops. Delighted when someone recommends something great.

**What Cru gives them:** A personal guide that learns what they love, tells them why they love it, and shows them where to get it nearby — without ever making them feel dumb.

## Persona 2: The Enthusiast (Secondary)

**Who:** 35-55, has a wine fridge or a small collection (50-200 bottles). Reads Wine Spectator or follows natural wine Instagram accounts. Spends $30-100/bottle regularly.

**Motivation:** "I want to discover what's next before everyone else does, and I want a better way to manage what I already have."

**Current behavior:** Uses CellarTracker for inventory, WineBid for occasional purchases, follows somms on social media. Frustrated that these tools don't talk to each other.

**What Cru gives them:** Unified cellar management, access to curated rare bottles, a secondary market to trade, and a community of people who share their obsession without the pretension.

## Persona 3: The Collector/Investor (Tertiary)

**Who:** 40-65, high-net-worth. Collection of 500+ bottles. Treats wine as both passion and alternative asset.

**Motivation:** "I want to enjoy wine AND have my collection work for me financially."

**What Cru gives them:** Professional cellaring with monitoring and alerts, investment analytics, fractional collection clubs, and the ability to consign bottles for sale on a liquid marketplace.

## Persona 4: The Retailer/Partner (Supply-Side)

**Who:** Independent wine shops, boutique importers, small-production wineries.

**Motivation:** "I want to reach customers who actually care about what I'm selling, not just discount shoppers."

**What Cru gives them:** A demand channel of pre-qualified, curious buyers shopping by story and taste profile. Live inventory integration. Local delivery/pickup coordination.

# 4. Core Value Proposition

## For Explorers

You had that glass on a rooftop in Barcelona, at a farmhouse dinner in Sonoma, at your friend's kitchen table — and it changed something. You didn't just like it. You wanted to know it. Who made it, where, why it tasted like that. Cru is the platform that takes that moment and turns it into a permanent way of buying wine. You tell us what moved you. We learn your palate. And we show you what's out there — bottles from obsessive producers in places you've never heard of, at prices that don't require a second thought, available at a shop ten minutes from your house. This isn't wine education. This is wine matchmaking.

## For Enthusiasts & Collectors

You've outgrown the apps. You're tracking bottles on spreadsheets, bidding on one platform, storing with another, and finding recommendations on Instagram from people you've never met. Cru consolidates the entire lifecycle — discover, acquire, trade, cellar, invest, share — into one platform built by people who understand that a collection is both an obsession and an asset. Stop stitching together tools that weren't built for you.

## For Retailers & Producers

The big-box stores compete on price. You compete on curation and care. Cru sends you buyers who are specifically looking for what you carry — people shopping by story, by producer, by palate fit. Not coupon clippers. Your kind of customer.

## The One-Liner

Cru is the anti-elitist wine marketplace — where AI-powered personal curation meets real human expertise to connect everyday drinkers with extraordinary wines, local retailers with the right customers, and collectors with the infrastructure their passion deserves.

# 5. Product Principles

These rules govern every product decision. When in doubt, the team checks these:

1. **Curiosity over expertise.** We never assume the user knows anything about wine. We never make them feel like they should. Every interaction should spark curiosity, not quiz knowledge.
2. **Stories over scores.** A 92-point rating means nothing to our primary user. Who made this wine, why is it special, and why might I love it — that's what sells bottles and builds relationships.
3. **Accessible doesn't mean dumbed down.** We don't simplify wine. We translate it. The depth is there for anyone who wants it; the surface is welcoming for everyone.
4. **Local first.** The best wine experience ends at a store you can walk into or a delivery you can track. We connect digital discovery to physical purchase.
5. **The farmer earns the spotlight.** Small producers who do exceptional work get priority visibility. We are not a platform for marketing budgets to dominate.
6. **One platform, every level of engagement.** Whether you're buying your first bottle over $15 or managing a $100K collection, Cru grows with you. No separate apps, no tiered gatekeeping.
7. **Community through the glass.** Wine is social. Every feature should create opportunities for connection — not isolation in an algorithm.

# 7. Business Model

## Revenue Architecture: Hybrid Model

Three revenue vectors, each de-risking the others.

### Consumer Subscription Tiers

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free (Explorer) | $0 | Discovery, curation, local ordering, community |
| Cru+ (Enthusiast) | $49/year | Cellar management, advanced recs, trading access, price alerts |
| Cru Vault (Collector) | $249/year | Investment analytics, cellaring integration, consignment, fractional access |

### Marketplace Commissions

| Transaction Type | Commission |
|-----------------|------------|
| Retail order (local fulfillment) | 8-15% |
| P2P secondary market trades | 10-15% (buyer + seller) |
| Auction hammer fee | 15-20% buyer premium |
| Consignment sales | 20-30% of sale price |
| DTC producer sales | 12-18% |

### B2B SaaS (P1+)

| Partner Type | Model |
|-------------|-------|
| Retailer monthly fee | $99-299/mo for dashboard, analytics, demand signals |
| Producer storefront fee | $149-499/mo for DTC infrastructure on Cru |

**Pricing philosophy:** Start lean, expand as distribution provides more value. Subscription exists to segment users and fund development, not as the primary revenue engine. Marketplace commissions and B2B fees are the growth drivers.

# 8. Competitive Landscape

## Market Context

Global wine market: $358B (2025), growing at 5.8% CAGR. US wine e-commerce: $0.8B → $11.8B projected by 2034 (31.7% CAGR). The opportunity is massive, but current players each own a piece without delivering the whole picture.

## Competitive Positioning

| Competitor | Strength | Gap | Cru's Advantage |
|-----------|----------|-----|-----------------|
| Vivino (55M users) | Scale, crowd-sourced ratings | Ratings are not curation. No storytelling, no cellar, no trading | Cru curates by palate fit and story, not crowd noise |
| Wine.com / Total Wine | Large catalog, reliable delivery | Transactional. No discovery engine. No community | Cru makes discovery the product |
| CellarTracker | Best cellar management, 13M reviews | No commerce, dated UX, steep learning curve | Cru integrates cellar into a full ecosystem |
| Naked Wines | Mission-driven, DTC producer support | Subscription-only, limited selection | Cru supports producers without locking consumers into boxes |
| Sotheby's / WineBid | Prestige, rare bottle access | High fees, slow auctions, no daily buying integration | Cru offers continuous liquidity alongside everyday discovery |
| Vinovest | Fractional wine investment, low $1K minimum | Investment-only. No drinking, no community | Cru treats wine as both passion and asset |
| Drizly (defunct 2025) | Established delivery behavior | Dead. Unit economics failed | Cru routes to local retailers — capital-light model |

## Strategic Moat

No player offers the full stack — discovery + local commerce + secondary market + cellaring + investment + community. They're all point solutions. Cru is the first vertically integrated wine platform serving every engagement level.

The hybrid AI + human curation engine, combined with community taste signal data, creates a compounding data advantage that's nearly impossible to replicate.

# 9. Go-to-Market Strategy

## Phase 1: Single City Blitz (Months 1-3)

**Target:** Los Angeles — massive wine culture, strong natural wine scene, density of independent shops, proximity to Paso Robles/Santa Barbara wine country.

**Supply-side (Weeks 1-6):**
Sign 20 independent wine shops in target neighborhoods (Silver Lake, Highland Park, Venice, Downtown). Onboard 30-50 boutique producers with compelling stories — focus on natural wine, women-owned, BIPOC-owned, single-vineyard. Recruit 5 founding curators (working sommeliers with 5K+ social followings).

**Demand-side (Weeks 4-12):**
Launch event — "Cru Tasting" pop-up with flagship retail partners. 200-person invite list of food/wine micro-influencers. Instagram + TikTok content engine via curator accounts (3 posts/day). Geo-targeted paid acquisition at less than $8 CPA. Referral mechanic: $10 credit per friend who purchases.

**Month 3 milestone:** 5,000 active users, 200+ orders/week, 20 retail partners live, NPS >50.

## Phase 2: Expansion Playbook (Months 4-8)

**City 2 (Month 4):** San Francisco — natural wine capital, Napa/Sonoma proximity.
**City 3 (Month 6):** New York — largest US wine market, media capital.

Earned growth channels unlocking: SEO via producer story pages, community-driven organic sharing, wine media coverage (VinePair, Punch, Eater, Bon Appétit), DTC producer launch for national reach.

**Month 8 milestone:** 25,000 active users, 1,000+ orders/week, 60 retail partners, 100+ producers.

## Phase 3: Scale (Months 9-18)

Secondary marketplace and auctions go live. National DTC expansion. Retailer self-serve onboarding. Premium subscription tiers launch. Content partnerships with wine educators.

**Month 18 milestone:** 100K+ active users, 5,000+ orders/week, 200+ retail partners, 500+ producers, $2M+ ARR run rate, Series A ready.

# 10. Technical Architecture Summary

Cru is built on a modern, scalable stack designed for multi-tenancy, real-time commerce, and AI-driven personalization.

| Layer | Technology |
|-------|-----------|
| Framework | Next.js 15 (App Router) with React Server Components |
| Database | Supabase (PostgreSQL with Row Level Security) |
| Commerce Engine | Medusa.js v2 (headless e-commerce) |
| Styling | Tailwind CSS + Shadcn/ui |
| Auth | Supabase Auth (SSO/SAML 2.0) |
| Payments | Stripe (Checkout, Portal, Webhooks) |
| Deployment | Vercel with preview deploys |
| Monorepo | Turborepo |
| Testing | Vitest, Playwright, pgTAP, axe-core |

The architecture supports the full feature roadmap from P0 through P3, with multi-tenant isolation enabling B2B extensions (retailer dashboards, producer portals) without re-architecture.
